Privacy Policy — Driver Companion

Last updated: 17 June 2026

Driver Companion ("the app") is built to help rideshare drivers quickly judge whether an incoming trip offer is worth accepting. This policy explains what the app does and does not do with your information.

What the app does

Driver Companion uses Android's Accessibility Service to read the text shown on screen when a trip offer appears from a supported rideshare app (such as Uber, Bolt, or FreeNow). It uses on-device text recognition (OCR) to extract details like fare, distance, time, and rider rating, then shows you a colour-coded border and an optional floating card with calculated earnings rates (£/min, £/mile, £/hr) so you can decide whether to accept the trip.

What the app does not do

  • It does not accept, reject, or otherwise interact with trip offers on your behalf.
  • It does not control or interact with any other app.
  • It does not record your screen, take photos, or save screenshots permanently — screenshots are processed in memory for OCR and then discarded.
  • It does not access personal data such as your name, contacts, messages, or photos.
  • It does not require or use an account, login, or sign-in of any kind.

Data collection and storage

Driver Companion does not collect, transmit, or share any data with the developer or any third party. The app makes no network requests. All information — including shift history, earnings inputs, mileage, and notes you choose to add — is stored locally on your device only, using standard Android app storage. Uninstalling the app permanently deletes this data.

The app does not use analytics, crash reporting, advertising, or any other third-party service that would send data off your device.

Permissions used

  • Accessibility Service — used solely to read on-screen trip offer text from supported rideshare apps, so the app can calculate and display earnings information. This permission is not used for any other purpose.
  • Display over other apps — used to show the coloured border and floating verdict card on top of your rideshare app.
  • Notifications — used for app status alerts.
  • Battery optimization exemption — used so the app continues running reliably in the background while you are driving, without Android pausing it to save power.

The app does not interact with, modify, or interfere with any rideshare platform's app, account, or services — it only displays calculated information based on what is already shown on your own screen.

Driver Companion is intended for use by adult rideshare drivers and is not directed at children. It does not knowingly collect information from children.
